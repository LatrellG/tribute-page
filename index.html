George Washington Carver

A timeline of some of his signifigant life events

  • 1864: George Washington Carver is born into slavery in Diamond, Missouri.
  • 1870: The 13th Amendment to the United States Constitution is ratified, abolishing slavery.
  • 1876: Carver moves to Neosho, Missouri, to attend a school for African American children.
  • 1880: Carver graduates from Minneapolis High School in Minneapolis, Kansas.
  • 1887: Carver enrolls at Simpson College in Indianola, Iowa, to study art and piano.
  • 1890: Carver transfers to Iowa State Agricultural College (now Iowa State University) to study agriculture.
  • 1896: Carver graduates from Iowa State Agricultural College with a Bachelor of Science degree in Agriculture.
  • 1897-1898: Carver serves as an assistant botanist at the Iowa Agricultural Experiment Station.
  • 1898: Carver receives a Master of Science degree in Agriculture from Iowa State Agricultural College.
  • 1898-1899: Carver studies at the Iowa State College Graduate School.
  • 1899-1900: Carver studies at the Tuskegee Institute in Tuskegee, Alabama, under the guidance of its founder, Booker T. Washington.
  • 1901: Carver becomes director of agricultural research at Tuskegee Institute, where he spends the rest of his career.
  • 1916: Carver becomes a member of the Royal Society of Arts in London, England.
  • 1921: Carver testifies before the United States Congress in support of a peanut tariff, arguing that it would benefit both farmers and consumers.
  • 1923: Carver is made a member of the British Society of Arts and is awarded the Spingarn Medal by the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP).
  • 1938: Carver's health declines, and he is hospitalized.
  • 1940: George Washington Carver dies on January 5th in Tuskegee, Alabama.
